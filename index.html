Common questions

Questions & answers

What age do you teach?

I teach students from age 5 upwards, through to adults of any age. Younger children (under 7 or 8) generally start with 30-minute sessions to keep things focused and fun, building up to longer lessons as they progress.

Do I need a piano at home to practise on?

Access to a piano or keyboard at home is strongly recommended so students can practise between lessons — this is where the real progress happens. A basic keyboard with weighted or semi-weighted keys is perfectly suitable for beginners and doesn't need to be expensive.

How long are the lessons?

Lessons are available in 30-minute and 60-minute slots. 30-minute lessons are recommended for younger children and complete beginners; 60-minute lessons suit older students, those preparing for exams, or anyone who wants to cover more ground each session.

Can adults learn piano with you?

Absolutely — adult learners are very welcome, whether you're a complete beginner, returning to the piano after a break, or working towards a particular goal. Lessons are entirely tailored to your pace and interests, with no pressure or judgement.

Do you prepare students for ABRSM exams?

Yes. I have extensive experience preparing students for ABRSM graded examinations across all levels. Exam preparation is always woven into lessons in a way that is structured but also enjoyable — the goal is always confident, musical playing, not just passing the test.

What happens if I need to cancel a lesson?

I understand that things come up — just get in touch as early as possible and we'll always try to find a solution.
